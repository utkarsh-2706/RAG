TOPIC: vector_databases
TEXT: Vector databases store high-dimensional embeddings and enable fast approximate nearest neighbor search. They are the backbone of modern RAG systems. Popular options include Pinecone, Weaviate, Qdrant, and FAISS.
---
CHUNK_ID: 2
TOPIC: embeddings
TEXT: Embeddings are dense numerical representations of text. They capture semantic meaning, allowing similar concepts to have similar vectors. Models like OpenAI's text-embedding-ada-002 or sentence-transformers generate these embeddings.
---
CHUNK_ID: 3
TOPIC: retrieval
TEXT: Retrieval in RAG systems is fundamentally a ranking problem. Given a query, the system ranks all document chunks by relevance score and returns the top K. The quality of retrieval directly determines the quality of generated answers.
---
CHUNK_ID: 4
TOPIC: retrieval
TEXT: Top-K retrieval selects the K most similar chunks based on cosine similarity between the query embedding and document embeddings. The value of K controls the tradeoff between precision (fewer, more relevant chunks) and recall (more chunks, higher chance of finding the answer).
---
CHUNK_ID: 5
TOPIC: chunking
TEXT: Chunking is the process of splitting documents into smaller pieces before embedding. Chunk size affects retrieval quality — too small loses context, too large introduces noise. Common strategies include fixed-size, sentence-based, and semantic chunking.
---
CHUNK_ID: 6
TOPIC: retrieval
TEXT: A common failure mode in retrieval is returning duplicate or near-duplicate chunks. This happens when the same information appears multiple times in the corpus, causing Top-K to return redundant results and wasting context window space.
---
CHUNK_ID: 7
TOPIC: llm_generation
TEXT: Large language models generate answers by conditioning on retrieved context. The quality of the generation depends heavily on what was retrieved — irrelevant or noisy chunks confuse the model and lead to hallucinations or wrong answers.
---
CHUNK_ID: 8
TOPIC: retrieval
TEXT: Cosine similarity measures the angle between two vectors, not their magnitude. A score of 1.0 means identical direction (highly similar), 0.0 means orthogonal (unrelated), and -1.0 means opposite. Most semantic similarities fall between 0.6 and 0.95.
---
CHUNK_ID: 9
TOPIC: hybrid_search
TEXT: Hybrid search combines sparse retrieval (BM25, keyword-based) with dense retrieval (embeddings). BM25 is excellent for exact keyword matches, while dense retrieval handles semantic similarity. Together they cover each other's weaknesses.
---
CHUNK_ID: 10
TOPIC: reranking
TEXT: Reranking is a post-retrieval step where a more powerful cross-encoder model rescores the top-K retrieved chunks. It improves precision significantly but adds latency. Commonly used when retrieval alone is not accurate enough.
---
CHUNK_ID: 11
TOPIC: retrieval
TEXT: The precision-recall tradeoff in retrieval: a small K gives high precision (returned chunks are very relevant) but low recall (might miss the right chunk). A large K gives high recall but low precision (many irrelevant chunks enter the context).
---
CHUNK_ID: 12
TOPIC: embeddings
TEXT: Embedding models are trained using contrastive learning — similar sentences are pushed close together in vector space, dissimilar ones are pushed apart. The quality of the embedding model directly affects retrieval performance in RAG.
---
CHUNK_ID: 13
TOPIC: vector_databases
TEXT: FAISS (Facebook AI Similarity Search) is an open-source library for efficient similarity search. It supports both exact and approximate nearest neighbor search, making it suitable for both small datasets and billion-scale production systems.
---
CHUNK_ID: 14
TOPIC: retrieval
TEXT: Metadata filtering allows retrieval to be scoped to specific subsets of a corpus. For example, filtering by document date, author, category, or language before applying vector similarity. This dramatically improves precision in large, mixed corpora.
